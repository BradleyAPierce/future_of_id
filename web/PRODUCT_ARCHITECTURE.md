# Purpose

This document defines the product architecture of Future of ID.

Its purpose is to clarify:

- what the platform is
- who it serves
- what content exists
- how learning experiences are organized
- where AI fits into the platform
- how the platform can scale over time

This document should guide:

- product decisions
- information architecture
- navigation
- design system work
- Figma planning
- future feature development

---

# Product Definition

Future of ID is a capability development system demonstrated through a guided professional learning platform. The current product implements Bradley's seven-capability pathway as the first complete demonstration of the reusable method and learning architecture.

The platform demonstrates capability development by applying its own reusable method and learning architecture.

Future of ID combines:

- professional learning
- instructional design practice
- AI-assisted learning experiences
- modern web application design
- full-stack product development

---

# Product Vision

Help instructional designers move from:

text Course Builders

to:

text Learning System Designers

The platform should help learners understand not only what is changing in instructional design, but how to work effectively within those changes.

# Core Product Pillars

## Pillar 1

Learning Science

Future-facing ideas should remain grounded in credible instructional design practice.

Examples:

- ADDIE
- SAM
- Kirkpatrick
- Experiential Learning
- Deliberate Practice
- Performance Improvement

---

## Pillar 2

AI-Augmented Design

AI is treated as a capability, not a replacement for instructional thinking.

Examples:

- AI feedback
- AI reflection support
- AI coaching
- AI simulations
- AI-assisted design workflows

---

## Pillar 3

Systems Thinking

Instructional design is increasingly becoming a systems discipline.

Examples:

- content systems
- learning ecosystems
- workflow automation
- analytics
- personalization

---

## Pillar 4

Practice and Application

Learners should apply concepts, not merely read about them.

Examples:

- scenarios
- simulations
- decision-making activities
- reflections
- AI-supported feedback
