LANDING_PAGE_STRATEGY.md

Purpose

The Future of ID landing page is not intended to function as a marketing splash page, trend report, or AI hype site.

Its primary purpose is to clearly explain:

* what Future of ID is,
* what it is not,
* who it serves,
* why it exists,
* and how the platform approaches instructional design in the context of AI-enabled workflows and modern learning systems.

The landing page should establish credibility through transparency, clarity, and practical positioning rather than through futurist language or exaggerated claims.

⸻

Core Positioning

Primary Positioning Statement

Future of ID is a practical exploration of how instructional design work is evolving through AI-enabled workflows, modern development systems, and transparent design practice.

The platform documents real projects, experiments, workflows, decisions, constraints, and lessons learned from actual implementation work.

Future of ID is not attempting to predict the future of instructional design. It is documenting one practitioner’s evolving experience working inside changes that are already happening.

⸻

Important Positioning Clarification

Future of ID does not assume that every instructional designer needs the same level of technical depth.

Instructional design roles vary significantly depending on:

* company size,
* industry,
* learning team structure,
* technology environment,
* business priorities,
* and personal career interests.

The platform should clearly communicate that AI expands the range of possibilities available to instructional designers, but not every instructional designer needs to follow the same path.

Examples:

* Some instructional designers may only use AI to improve writing workflows, learning objectives, voice-over scripts, quizzes, or storyboards.
* Others may use AI to improve advanced authoring workflows in tools like Articulate Storyline, Rise, Adobe Captivate, or iSpring.
* Others may explore simulations, AI-assisted learner feedback, or chatbot integrations.
* Others may move toward more technical implementations involving APIs, vector databases, guardrails, orchestration workflows, or custom-built learning systems.

None of these paths are presented as “correct” or mandatory.

Future of ID represents one evolving implementation path based on:

* the creator’s professional role,
* organizational environment,
* technical interests,
* systems-thinking approach,
* and real-world project responsibilities.

The platform should encourage instructional designers to determine what level of AI adoption and technical exploration is appropriate for their own work.

⸻

What Future of ID Is

Future of ID is:

* a practical learning platform,
* a transparent design record,
* a documented implementation space,
* a working exploration of AI-enabled instructional design,
* and a place to study real workflows, decisions, tradeoffs, and build processes.

The platform focuses on:

* practical application,
* systems thinking,
* instructional design judgment,
* implementation realities,
* architecture decisions,
* workflow evolution,
* and responsible AI usage.

The platform values:

* transparency over performance,
* implementation over prediction,
* practical insight over trend commentary,
* and documented experience over abstract theory.

⸻

What Future of ID Is Not

Future of ID is not:

* a prediction platform,
* a thought leadership brand,
* an AI trend commentary site,
* a replacement for instructional design fundamentals,
* or a claim that all instructional designers must become software developers.

The platform should avoid:

* exaggerated futurist claims,
* “AI will replace instructional designers” messaging,
* hype-driven language,
* vague transformation language,
* generic productivity claims,
* and shallow AI commentary disconnected from real implementation work.

The platform should also avoid implying that:

* technical complexity equals professional value,
* all instructional designers need coding skills,
* or all IDs should pursue advanced AI system architecture.

⸻

Homepage Communication Goals

The homepage should quickly communicate:

1. What Future of ID actually is.
2. Why the platform exists.
3. Who the platform is for.
4. How the platform approaches AI and instructional design.
5. The difference between practical implementation and AI hype culture.
6. That this platform documents real work, not abstract predictions.
7. That instructional designers can engage with AI at different levels of depth.

⸻

Tone and Voice Strategy

Tone Goals

The landing page should feel:

* practical,
* grounded,
* transparent,
* thoughtful,
* credible,
* systems-oriented,
* calm,
* and implementation-focused.

The tone should avoid:

* sounding like a startup pitch,
* sounding like a futurist manifesto,
* sounding like LinkedIn thought leadership,
* sounding overly academic,
* or sounding like AI marketing copy.

The writing should feel like:

* an experienced practitioner explaining real work,
* a transparent builder sharing observations,
* and a systems thinker documenting evolving workflows.

⸻

Messaging Principles

Observed, Not Predicted

The platform shares:

* what has been built,
* what has been tested,
* what has been learned,
* and what has been observed through implementation.

The platform does not attempt to forecast the future of the industry.

⸻

Built, Not Theorized

The platform prioritizes:

* real systems,
* real workflows,
* real projects,
* and practical implementation details.

The work itself should function as evidence.

⸻

Transparent, Not Performative

The platform should openly discuss:

* tradeoffs,
* limitations,
* architecture decisions,
* failed experiments,
* workflow challenges,
* and where human judgment remains critical.

⸻

Useful, Not Trendy

The goal is not to impress users with AI terminology or complexity.

The goal is to help instructional designers:

* think more clearly,
* evaluate tools responsibly,
* understand evolving workflows,
* and identify practical approaches relevant to their own work.

Suggested Homepage Structure

1. Hero Section

Purpose:
Immediately establish what Future of ID is and is not.

Potential messaging direction:

* The future of instructional design is being built, not predicted.
* Practical instructional design systems, workflows, and AI-enabled learning experiences.
* Real implementation work from one evolving practitioner path.

Primary CTA:

* Explore the Platform
* Start Learning
* Explore the Modules

Secondary CTA:

* Read the Philosophy
* Explore the Build Process
* View Reverse Builds

⸻

2. What This Platform Is

Explain:

* practical exploration,
* implementation focus,
* AI-enabled workflows,
* transparency,
* and evolving instructional design practice.

This section should establish credibility through specificity rather than broad claims.

⸻

3. Different Paths for Different Instructional Designers

This is a critical section.

Purpose:
Clarify that instructional designers may engage with AI and technology at very different levels.

Potential examples:

* AI-assisted writing workflows
* Storyline/Rise enhancement workflows
* Interactive simulations
* AI-assisted learner support
* Chatbots and feedback systems
* Learning system architecture
* Custom platform development

The section should communicate:

* flexibility,
* optionality,
* and professional diversity.

⸻

4. Why This Platform Exists

Explain:

* the gap between AI hype and implementation reality,
* the lack of transparent workflow discussions,
* and the need for grounded practitioner-based examples.

Potential themes:

* AI makes content generation easier, but not design judgment.
* Instructional designers still need to make decisions, evaluate outputs, and structure learning responsibly.
* The profession is evolving unevenly across organizations and roles.

⸻

5. Platform Philosophy

Potential principles:

* Observed, not predicted
* Built, not theorized
* Transparent, not performative
* Useful, not trendy

This section should establish long-term platform credibility.

⸻

6. What Users Will Find Inside

Potential categories:

* AI literacy for instructional designers
* AI-assisted workflows
* Modern learning architecture
* Reflection and scenario systems
* Reverse Build design breakdowns
* Storyline/Rise workflow examples
* Learning platform implementation examples
* Practical workflow experiments
* Real-world instructional design tradeoffs

⸻

7. Final Positioning Close

The closing message should reinforce:

* transparency,
* practical implementation,
* evolving workflows,
* and responsible exploration.

Potential positioning direction:

Future of ID is not about predicting the future of instructional design.

It is about documenting what instructional design work looks like while the profession is actively changing.

⸻

Design Direction Notes

The landing page should visually reflect:

* clarity,
* structure,
* calm confidence,
* and modern systems thinking.

Avoid:

* excessive animations,
* startup-style gradients everywhere,
* AI-themed visual clichés,
* neon “future” aesthetics,
* or overly corporate marketing visuals.

The interface should feel:

* organized,
* intentional,
* modular,
* readable,
* and professionally grounded.

⸻

Long-Term Strategic Goal

The landing page should become the platform’s credibility anchor.

It should help users understand that Future of ID is:

* an evolving implementation record,
* a practical instructional design resource,
* and a transparent exploration of how AI-enabled workflows may expand the possibilities available to instructional designers.

The homepage should make it clear that:

* there is no single “correct” future for instructional design,
* different professionals will adopt different levels of AI integration,
* and the platform exists to help instructional designers explore those possibilities responsibly.
