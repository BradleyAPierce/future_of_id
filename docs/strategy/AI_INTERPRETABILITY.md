# Future of ID — AI Interpretability Strategy

## Purpose

AI interpretability means designing pages, frameworks, and learning experiences so both humans and AI assistants can accurately understand and explain them.

Future of ID should be clear enough for a learner to understand directly and structured enough for an AI/browser assistant to summarize, navigate, and teach without inventing missing context.

This is not an SEO document, technical AI implementation document, or prompt-engineering guide. It is a product experience and learning-design strategy document.

## Why This Matters

Learners may increasingly use browser AI to ask questions such as:

> What is this page teaching me?

AI assistants need clear structure, named concepts, explicit relationships, and concise takeaways in order to explain a page accurately.

A page that is clear enough for AI to explain is often clearer for humans too. When concepts are named, relationships are visible, and learner questions are explicit, both people and AI assistants are less likely to misread the experience.

Future of ID should be prepared for AI-mediated learning experiences where learners use browser assistants to summarize frameworks, compare sections, clarify concepts, or decide where to start.

## Core Principle

Future of ID should not only be readable by humans. It should be teachable by AI.

Good instructional design helps humans think. Clear instructional design also helps AI interpret.

## What Makes a Page AI-Interpretable

An AI-interpretable page should include:

- clear headings
- named frameworks
- explicit learner questions
- one-sentence takeaways
- visible relationships between concepts
- minimal ambiguity
- page sections with distinct psychological jobs
- framework graphics supported by nearby explanatory text
- avoidance of vague hype language
- concepts that do not rely entirely on unstated context

The goal is not to write for machines instead of people. The goal is to make the learning experience structured enough that a human or AI assistant can explain it in the same basic way.

## Framework Interpretability Pattern

Every major framework should include:

- Framework name
- Transformational question
- Mental model
- Emotional job
- Memorable line
- AI explanation summary

The AI explanation summary should be short enough that a browser assistant could accurately explain the framework to a new learner.

Example:

```text
Experience in Action shows that Future of ID teaches through practice, AI-supported feedback, reflection, and Reverse Build. The learner does not only complete an activity; they also inspect why the activity was designed that way.
```

## AI-Assisted Browsing Scenario

A learner opens the Future of ID homepage and asks a browser AI:

> I'm new to instructional design. What is this page trying to teach me? What do these frameworks mean? Where should I start?

Future of ID should support this kind of interaction.

The page should give the assistant enough structure to explain:

- what Future of ID is
- what the major frameworks mean
- how the learner can begin
- why AI is discussed as support for judgment, not replacement for judgment
- how Orientation, Dashboard, modules, Reverse Build, and reflection relate

The learner should receive an explanation that matches the product's intent because the page itself makes that intent visible.

## Design Implications

- Orientation should introduce frameworks clearly enough for AI and humans.
- Pages should make relationships explicit rather than assuming prior navigation.
- Modules should be internally understandable because learners may enter anywhere.
- Reverse Build should be self-explanatory and not depend on sequence.
- Frameworks should function as reusable mental models, not decorative graphics.
- AI summaries should not replace human-facing content, but should reinforce clarity.

AI interpretability should make Future of ID more understandable, not more verbose. The product should use concise language, clear relationships, and named concepts to reduce confusion.

## Quality Review Questions

- Could a browser AI accurately explain this page to a first-time learner?
- Could it explain the purpose of each framework without inventing context?
- Are the key learner questions visible?
- Are section purposes clear?
- Does the page avoid relying on a linear path?
- Does the page support both AI-curious and AI-skeptical learners?
- Is there enough context for a learner who enters from search, social, or a direct link?
