# Future of ID — Instructional Architecture

## Purpose

Future of ID intentionally teaches through multiple instructional layers.

Each layer has a unique instructional responsibility. No layer exists to duplicate another. Together, the layers create one coherent professional learning experience that helps instructional designers move from understanding ideas to practicing judgment and transferring that judgment into work.

This document defines how Future of ID teaches. It is not an implementation document, React architecture document, or curriculum outline.

## Core Philosophy

Future of ID does not rely on a single instructional strategy.

Instead, it layers complementary learning experiences that move learners from understanding, to judgment, to practice, to professional growth.

The architecture teaches learners:

- how to think
- how to decide
- how to practice
- how experienced designers reason
- how to transfer those ideas into professional work

## Instructional Architecture

### Layer 1 — Learning Frameworks

Purpose:
Change how learners understand the profession.

Examples:

- Learning System
- Human Judgment in the Age of AI
- Different Paths
- Experience in Action
- Stronger Together
- Reverse Build

Role:
Learning Frameworks are enduring mental models. They answer why.

They help learners understand what kind of professional practice Future of ID is preparing them to enter.

### Layer 2 — Situational Mental Models

Purpose:
Support one professional decision.

Examples:

- From Request to Diagnosis
- Evidence -> Interpretation -> Decision -> Measurement
- Learner Difference -> Evidence -> Trigger -> Response -> Review
- Messy Content -> Structured Objects -> Reuse Rules -> Governance

Role:
Situational Mental Models are compact professional reasoning tools. They answer how.

They help learners make one decision more clearly without adding unnecessary theory or visual weight.

### Layer 3 — Design Practice

Purpose:
Provide authentic opportunities to exercise professional judgment.

Role:
Design Practice asks learners to make meaningful instructional design decisions rather than complete traditional quizzes.

The learner should reason through ambiguity, constraints, trade-offs, and professional consequences.

### Layer 4 — AI Design Review

Purpose:
Critique learner reasoning.

Role:
AI functions as an instructional design reviewer rather than a chatbot or grading engine.

It should help learners sharpen judgment, test assumptions, and improve transfer. Professional judgment remains central.

### Layer 5 — Reverse Build

Purpose:
Reveal professional reasoning.

Role:
Reverse Build helps learners reconstruct the learning experience they just completed from the instructional designer's perspective.

It teaches learners how experienced designers think by making the rationale, constraints, trade-offs, AI collaboration, and learning science visible.

### Layer 6 — Reflection & Transfer

Purpose:
Help learners transfer professional reasoning into their own work.

Role:
Reflection is not course recap.

Reflection is future professional application. It asks learners what they now see differently, what judgment they will carry forward, and how the experience should change their next design decision.

## Layer Relationships

The instructional layers work as a connected sequence:

```text
Learning Frameworks
↓
Situational Mental Models
↓
Design Practice
↓
AI Design Review
↓
Reverse Build
↓
Reflection & Transfer
```

Every layer supports the next.

Learning Frameworks orient the learner. Situational Mental Models give the learner a practical decision tool. Design Practice creates a meaningful judgment moment. AI Design Review critiques that reasoning. Reverse Build reveals why the experience was designed that way. Reflection & Transfer helps the learner carry the reasoning into future work.

None of these layers replaces another. The strength of Future of ID comes from the relationship between them.

## Quality Principles

- Every layer has one instructional purpose.
- Avoid duplication between layers.
- Reduce cognitive load.
- Reinforce professional judgment.
- Build on established instructional design foundations.
- Support AI interpretability.
- Every module should stand on its own.
- Every experience should contribute to a larger professional transformation.

## Future Vision

This architecture is intentionally independent of technology.

Whether learning experiences are built with React, Storyline, iSpring, AI simulations, or future technologies, the instructional architecture remains unchanged.

The technology delivers the experience.

The instructional architecture defines how the experience teaches.
